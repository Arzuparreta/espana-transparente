## Problem Statement

España Transparente has shipped substantial data infrastructure: 15+ tables, 19 public routes, ETL pipelines from Congreso, Senado, INE, PCSP, BDNS, BORME, Kohesio, Civio, BOE. Coverage is real: 350 deputies + 115 senators, 72.344 EU fund beneficiaries, 2016–2026 budgets, 63 institutional appointments, full puertas giratorias pipeline.

Two concurrent observations make a re-shaping move necessary now:

1. **Discovery is fragmented.** A Reddit visitor landing on `/` sees the home dashboard — Logo + 3 anchors (deuda/cápita, mayor contrato, IPC) + Gobierno + Votaciones + Puertas giratorias. The other 14 verticals (presupuestos, fondos UE, instituciones, subvenciones, indicadores, senado, contratos, organizaciones, iniciativas, partidos, distorsión, buscar, ministerios, estado-datos) live behind a navbar. Even a curious visitor leaves without knowing what is there. The product is **denser than its entry points suggest**.

2. **Real citizen feedback exposed a real gap, and a fast loop closed it.** On 2026-05-15, `davidroman2494` reported via r/ElusionFiscal that pensions were missing from `/presupuestos`. Commits `129b765` and `4df1306` shipped the data within ~48 hours. That feedback loop — citizen-reported expected-but-absent data → fast publication — is the canonical user-product interaction this project should run on. **The audit shows that case was almost certainly not unique** (see "Audit findings" below).

The next move is therefore two-layered:

- **Phase 0 — Audit-driven gap closure** (silent product debt now visible).
- **Phase 1 — Home re-anchoring + "qué hay aquí" index + source/freshness footers** (warmup, 1–2 days).
- **Phase 2 — Flagship trazabilidad funnel: "Cómo se gasta tu dinero"** (1–2 weeks, vision-aligned navigable cascade).

## What Makes This Cool

The integration *is* the product. `chispica` named this directly in the r/SpainEconomics thread: *"me imagino que ha tenido que ser una locura encontrar todas las fuentes de datos para esto."* Datos públicos in Spain live across hundreds of incompatible portals; consolidating them is the value. But that value is not visible to a first-time visitor — they only see one rectangle (the home) of a much bigger map.

The flagship move ("Cómo se gasta tu dinero") makes the consolidation *navigable*: a citizen starts from "tus impuestos", walks the chain — presupuesto → ministerio → ministro responsable → contratos/subvenciones/fondos UE asociados → organizaciones beneficiarias — and arrives at specific people and specific euros. **The vision triad (personas no abstracciones · la excepción es la información · trazabilidad radical) collapses into one flow.**

The cool part is that no editorial framing is required to make this powerful. The structure of the data *is* the argument. The user clicks → sees the chain → draws their own conclusion. This is the direct answer to the defamation moat: nothing in the funnel is opinion, every node links its official source.

---

## Constraints

- **No editorializing.** Hard rules in CLAUDE.md / AGENTS.md / `npm run content:audit` apply. Labels are factual. No "robado", no "expolio", no methodology text in UI.
- **No growth or audience optimization.** This is civic open-source. Success ≠ pageviews. Success = data is more complete, more navigable, more truthfully sourced. See `feedback-no-growth-coaching.md` memory.
- **The defamation reflex** (see `project-data-exposure-reception.md` memory) means source visibility is strategic defense, not polish. Every datum must be one click from the official portal it came from.
- **No paralelización al Congreso.** `REQUEST_DELAY = 1.5s` is non-negotiable. Senate ETL inherits this constraint.
- **Free-tier infra.** Supabase Free + Vercel Hobby; aggregations should be views or materialized views, not heavy on-request joins.
- **Editorial neutrality must hold even where the data is damning.** If the funnel surfaces "Ministerio X awarded EUR 200M to Org Y where minister Z later worked", the framing is data, not accusation. The `revolving_door` review gate is the model.

## Premises (agreed in session)

1. The David feedback loop ("I came for X, didn't find it, you shipped it in 2 days") is the canonical user-product interaction. Replicating it deliberately is more valuable than any acquisition move.
2. The site is currently denser than its entry points suggest.
3. The vision triad is under-represented at the front door (current home: deuda/cápita, mayor contrato, IPC — only "mayor contrato" partially expresses trazabilidad).
4. The defamation-reflex moat means strengthening source visibility and traceability is also strategic defense.
5. We do not optimize for subscribers, conversion, growth, social, virality, or audience. Moves are evaluated by: does this surface more data more truthfully, with more traceability, to whoever happens to be reading.

## Audit findings (Phase 0 input)

Full audit dispatched 2026-05-20 over the codebase. Cited results below; see audit report for evidence and file paths.

### A. Critical citizen-facing data gaps (David-class)

| # | Gap | Evidence | Citizen-facing impact |
|---|-----|----------|----------------------|
| A1 | **Senate voting not collected** | `etl/src/senado/senadores.py` scrapes the member list only; `votes` table has no rows with `chamber='senate'` | 115 senators visible without voting records → divergence detection only covers Congreso. The product implicitly claims "the legislature" but covers half of it. |
| A2 | **CCAA-level aggregation absent** | `nivel1='AUTONOMICA'` exists in subsidies; `responsibility_positions` covers regional officials. No `/ccaa` or `/comunidades-autonomas` route. | "¿Qué gasta la Generalitat / Junta de Andalucía / Gobierno Vasco?" → no answer. |
| A3 | **Municipal aggregation absent** | `awarding_body LIKE 'AYUNTAMIENTO DE %'` is queryable; `nivel1='LOCAL'` exists. No `/municipios` route. | "¿Qué gasta mi ayuntamiento?" → no answer. |
| A4 | **Multilevel responsibility chain invisible** | `responsibility_positions` (state/autonomic/municipal) is materialized in DB. Zero pages render it. | "¿Quién es responsable de este gasto?" → only state level is shown. |
| A5 | **Revenue side missing** | `budget_lines` are spending-side only (sección/capítulo). No tax collection / IRPF / IVA / sociedades ingestion. | "¿De dónde sale el dinero?" → no answer. (Larger lift; explicitly out of scope for this round, on the roadmap.) |
| A6 | **Initiatives list page missing** | `initiatives` table written by congreso ETL; `web/src/app/iniciativas/[id]/page.tsx` exists; no `/iniciativas` list page. The detail route is unreachable. | Iniciativas legislativas exist but cannot be browsed. |
| A7 | **Declarations no dedicated page** | `economic_declarations` has 1.149 records; only reachable via deputy detail or `/buscar`. No `/declaraciones` index. | Cannot browse who has reported what assets / interests. |

### B. Search-index gaps

`search_global()` covers only 5 entity types (politician, organization, voting_session, contract, revolving_door). `search_documents` materialized corpus is wider but inconsistent. Missing from at least one of them:

- B1. **parties** — cannot search "PP", "PSOE", "Vox", "Sumar" directly.
- B2. **institutional_appointments** — TC, CGPJ, RTVE, SEPI appointments invisible to search.
- B3. **eu_funds** — 72.344 beneficiaries; not searchable by recipient organization name in `search_global`.
- B4. **senate members** — searchable indirectly via `chamber='senate'` filter on politicians, but no dedicated entity-type tag in search_global.

### C. SEO / sitemap gaps

`web/src/app/sitemap.ts` includes only static + deputy/party routes. Missing 9 dynamic detail routes: `/votaciones/[id]`, `/partidos/[id]`, `/instituciones/[id]`, `/contratos/[id]`, `/subvenciones/[id]`, `/fondos-ue/[id]`, `/puertas-giratorias/[id]`, `/organizaciones/[id]`, `/indicadores/[code]`. These are not crawlable → permanent invisibility to anyone searching "contrato [X]" or "subvención [Y]" on Google. (This matters for civic infra: indexability is how the dato becomes findable, not for "traffic".)

### D. ETL safeguard weaknesses

- D1. `congreso/asistencia.py` has no `--resume`; full re-scrape each run. Brittle to Congress rate-limit.
- D2. `kohesio/fondos_ue.py` constrained by API serving only `offset=0`; ~30K of expected ~72K reachable per run. Documented but unresolved.
- D3. `senado/senadores.py` fragile to HTML structure changes (no fallback).
- D4. YAMLs in `etl/data/` mostly verified 2026-05-15; `party_leadership.yml` and `gobierno_historico.yml` should be monthly — drift check overdue at next session.

### E. Discovery / UX gaps

- E1. Home anchor "IPC mensual" is a generic INE chart — does not express any of the three vision principles.
- E2. No visible map of the 19 verticals. The navbar is the only entry into ~14 of them.
- E3. No per-section "fuente · última verificación · cobertura" footer. The defamation-reflex armor is missing on most pages.
- E4. `/estado-datos` exists but is not prominent.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Trazabilidad-first home + "qué hay aquí" map + source/freshness footers (warmup)

Summary: small UI re-shape. Replace `IPC mensual` hero anchor (E1) with a "divergencia destacada" widget. Add a categorized index of the 19 verticals as a `¿Qué hay aquí?` section on home. Add a per-section footer with `fuente oficial · última verificación · cobertura`. Fix sitemap (C).

Effort: S (1–2 days). All UI + small SQL view. No new ETL.
Risk: Low. Reversible.

Pros:
- Visible improvement to entry experience in days.
- Fixes the defamation-armor gap (E3) cheaply.
- Surfaces existing depth for first-time visitors.
- Sitemap fix is pure SEO upside.
Cons:
- Doesn't solve the deeper "siloed data" problem.
- Divergence widget needs an auto-pick rule to avoid being read as editorial.
Reuses: AnchorCard, SectionHeader, get_divergences(), existing nav config.

### Approach B: "Cómo se gasta tu dinero" flagship trazabilidad funnel

Summary: a new top-level page `/como-se-gasta` (or `/dinero-publico`, name TBD) that takes the visitor through the chain in a navigable cascade:

```
Presupuesto del año en vigor (2024 prórroga)
  ├─ Sección (Ministerio): Defensa, Educación, Sanidad, ...
  │   ├─ Ministro/a responsable (Persona, partido, cargo, fecha de nombramiento, fuente BOE)
  │   ├─ Programa presupuestario
  │   │   ├─ Contratos asociados (PCSP)
  │   │   ├─ Subvenciones asociadas (BDNS)
  │   │   └─ Fondos UE relacionados (Kohesio)
  │   │       └─ Organización beneficiaria
  │   │           ├─ Otros contratos
  │   │           ├─ Otras subvenciones
  │   │           └─ Personas vinculadas (admin, consejeros)
  │   │               └─ Puertas giratorias detectadas → ...
  └─ ...
```

Each node:
- Shows the data, not the framing.
- Links to its own existing detail page (`/contratos/[id]`, `/diputados/[id]`, etc.).
- Includes "fuente oficial · última verificación".
- Is searchable / shareable as a deep link.

Effort: M (1–2 weeks). UI synthesis + ~2 SQL views/joins. No new ETL.
Risk: Medium. Aggregations must not editorialize ("top despilfarro" framing forbidden).

Pros:
- Single navigable expression of the vision triad.
- Uses everything you've already paid to ingest (presupuestos, gobierno, contratos, subvenciones, fondos UE, responsables, puertas giratorias).
- Becomes the canonical share link (deep linkable per ministry / program).
- Directly answers the r/ElusionFiscal-style framing ("la gente que gasta tus impuestos") with raw data, no commentary.
- Strongest possible defense against "datos inventados": the chain is visible, every step sourced.

Cons:
- 1–2 weeks of focused work.
- Needs careful copy to read as navigable database, not essay.
- Visual hierarchy is non-trivial — cascade must work on mobile too.
Reuses: `budget_lines`, `v_budget_responsibility`, `contracts`, `grants` (subsidies), `eu_funds`, `organizations`, `politicians`, `responsibility_positions`, `revolving_door`. Possibly one new view `v_program_money_flow(year, section, program)` returning the cascaded rows.

### Approach C: "¿Falta un dato?" inline reporter + state-of-data dashboard

Summary: make the David loop a first-class product surface. Inline `¿Falta un dato aquí?` reporter on every section, writing to GitHub issues (already exists) or a moderation queue. Promote `/estado-datos` to a prominent home tile. Add a `qué entró esta semana` changelog with optional public-request attribution.

Effort: M (3–5 days). Small backend + UI insertions.
Risk: Low–Medium. Moderation is small ongoing cost.

Pros:
- Formalizes the only feedback channel that produces actual product improvement.
- "Última semana: pensiones contributivas (petición ciudadana, 2 días)" is strong armor against "datos inventados".
- Lightweight contributor adjacency.

Cons:
- Without B, the entry experience doesn't change.
- "A petición de X" risks slipping into promotional voice if not carefully framed.
Reuses: `annotations` table, `etl_runs`, `/estado-datos`, GitHub issues.

## Recommended Approach

**Phase 0 (audit gap closure) → Phase 1 (Approach A warmup) → Phase 2 (Approach B flagship).** Approach C deferred (the David loop already works via GitHub; formalizing it can wait).

Sequencing rationale:
- Phase 0 picks the must-fix gaps from the audit (A1, A2/A3, A6, B1, C, D1). Without them, building B on top would amplify silent omissions. Senate voting (A1) is the biggest single citizen-facing hole and must be closed before claiming "el legislativo" anywhere in the funnel.
- Phase 1 is so cheap it should ship in the first two days while A1 ETL design is being scoped.
- Phase 2 is the headline move and requires Phase 0 data to be honest.

## Open Questions

1. **Phase 2 entry point: new top-level route, or restructure `/`?** The flagship funnel could live at `/como-se-gasta-tu-dinero`, `/dinero-publico`, `/seguir-el-hilo`, or *become* the home. New route is safer (home stays scannable); funnel-as-home is more vision-aligned but bolder. *Recommendation: new top-level route first, evaluate in 4 weeks.*

2. **Senate voting (A1): scrape directly from the Senate portal, or wait for an open-data feed?** Need to investigate the Senate's voting publication format. Worst case: HTML per-session like Congreso. Best case: a CSV or XML feed.

3. **Divergence widget on home (Phase 1): which divergence to show?** Auto-pick by `divergence_count DESC` over last 30 days, or curated. *Recommendation: auto, with a clear "más reciente con N divergencias" label — no curation = no editorial risk.*

4. **CCAA / municipal pages (A2/A3): full pages or filters on existing pages?** Existing pages support filtering (`/subvenciones?nivel=AUTONOMICA`). Adding dedicated routes per CCAA (`/ccaa/cataluna`) means 17+ pages with their own SEO surface. *Open — depends on how complete the CCAA-level data is once aggregated.*

5. **Naming.** "Cómo se gasta tu dinero" is the working title and may already feel editorial. Alternatives: "El hilo del dinero público", "Trazabilidad del gasto", "Seguir el dinero". *Need user pick before Phase 2 design freeze.*

6. **YAML drift cadence (D4): is monthly cron-driven verification realistic or does it stay manual?**

## Success Criteria

A move is "done" when:

**Phase 0:**
- A1: every senator's voting record is in `votes` with `chamber='senate'`; `/senado/[id]` and divergence detection cover both chambers.
- A2/A3: at minimum, dedicated landing pages for CCAA-level and municipal-level spending exist (even if just well-faceted views over existing data).
- A6: `/iniciativas` list page is reachable and indexed.
- B1–B4: `search_global()` covers parties, institutional_appointments, eu_funds, and explicitly tags senate members.
- C: `sitemap.ts` includes all dynamic detail routes.
- D1: `congreso/asistencia.py` supports `--resume` via `etl_runs`.

**Phase 1:**
- Home shows a divergence widget instead of `IPC mensual`.
- `¿Qué hay aquí?` section lists all 19 verticals categorized.
- Every section page has a footer with `fuente · última verificación · cobertura`.

**Phase 2:**
- A new flagship route (name TBD) renders a navigable money-flow cascade.
- Every node in the cascade is deep-linkable.
- Every node shows source + last verified.
- A reader can travel from "tus impuestos" to a specific person + specific euro in ≤ 5 clicks.
- `npm run content:audit` and `npm run ui:audit` pass with no new violations.
