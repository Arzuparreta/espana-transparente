## Vision

> "I show you the relevant information the government does not want you to know.
> I help you understand what IPC, inflation, and public debt actually mean for your life."

España Transparente is two things simultaneously: an accountability tool for researchers and journalists,
and an economic literacy platform for citizens who never studied economics. Nobody else has built this
combination. The official transparency portal (`transparencia.gob.es`) is a compliance instrument, not
a product. The INE publishes numbers nobody can read. This project bridges both gaps.

---

## Problem Statement

Public money in Spain is traceable in theory and opaque in practice. Contracts, subsidies, EU funds,
asset declarations, and institutional appointments are scattered across incompatible official sources.
Meanwhile, the economic indicators that affect every citizen's daily life (IPC, salario medio, deuda
pública) are published as raw tables that require economics training to interpret.

The project's current state: solid pipelines for contracts, subsidies, EU funds, Senate/Congress votes,
institutional appointments, and revolving doors. Inconsistencies exist across some pipelines. Two data
dimensions are missing: structured asset/income declarations and SEPI state company boards. The indicators
section exists but doesn't explain anything.

---

## What Makes This Compelling

- The only Spanish portal that connects money → officials → organizations in one searchable place.
- The only place that tells your dad what IPC actually means for his rent and salary.
- All from official public sources — no editorializing, no partisan framing.

---

## Constraints

- Supabase Free (500 MB). No new heavy tables without auditing current usage first.
- `transparencia.gob.es` is the government's own portal — Spain has a documented pattern of
  labeling accountability journalism as partisan. Editorial neutrality is non-negotiable.
- Congress portal rate-limits at 1.5s/request. Declaration PDF parsing must be async and resumable.
- Vercel Hobby deployment. No server-side heavy compute.

---

## Premises (all confirmed)

1. `transparencia.gob.es` is not a competitor — it's a compliance tool, not a product.
2. España Transparente's value is integration: connecting records the government publishes in scattered, incompatible formats.
3. Economic indicators are only useful to citizens if explained in plain language, not just charted.
4. "Follow the money" only closes when you trace: company → contracts/subsidies/EU funds → officials who approved them.
5. Data gaps (declarations, SEPI) are additive, not blockers — but they must be documented and planned so nothing is forgotten.

---

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Economic Literacy Layer
Add plain-language explanations to `/indicadores`. No new pipelines. Front-end only.
- Effort: S (days). Risk: Low.
- Pros: immediate value for citizens; differentiates from every other transparency portal.
- Cons: doesn't close the "follow the money" vision yet.

### Approach B: Data Closure Sprint — Declarations + SEPI
Extend `etl/src/congreso/declaraciones.py` to fully parse income/asset PDFs.
Extend `etl/src/instituciones/instituciones.py` to cover SEPI boards more completely.
Surface on `/diputados/[id]` and `/organizaciones/[id]`.
- Effort: M (2-4 weeks). Risk: Medium (PDF parsing is brittle).
- Pros: closes the accountability gap; enables premise 4 to actually close.
- Cons: doesn't solve the citizen literacy problem alone.

### Approach C: Unified Entity Graph
Rearchitect search so any company or person returns a single unified view:
all money, all officials, all declarations, all institutional roles.
- Effort: L (4-8 weeks). Risk: High (complex cross-entity joins; hard UX).
- Pros: the actual vision. Journalist/researcher tool. The "whoa" moment.
- Cons: incomplete without A+B as foundations.

## Phase A — Economic Literacy (Ship First)

**Goal:** Make `/indicadores` useful to someone who never studied economics.

### Pre-conditions (verify before writing code)

- Confirm `salario_medio` annual series from 2020 exists in the `indicadores` table
  (or equivalent INE EPA table). If not present, the comparison chart (step 3) becomes
  a separate INE ingestion task — do not block Phase A on it.
- Audit the `/indicadores` page: list every indicator slug shown, its source table/column,
  and whether it has a time series back to at least 2020.

### What to build

1. **Contextual explanations on each indicator card.**
   The `/indicadores/[code]` detail page gets a 2-3 sentence plain-language explanation
   below the chart. The index cards in `IndicatorsDashboard` get a one-liner variant.
   Not an economics lecture. A specific statement:
   > "El IPC subió un 3.2% en 2025. Esto significa que algo que costaba 100€ en
   > enero de 2024 cuesta ahora 103.20€. Si tu salario no subió al menos un 3.2%,
   > tu poder de compra bajó."

   **Implementation:**
   - Create `web/src/lib/indicator-explanations.ts` with a record keyed by `indicator_code`.
     Each entry has `short` (one-liner for the index card) and `long` (2-3 sentences for
     the detail page).
   - In `/indicadores/[code]/page.tsx`, render `long` below the chart as a `<p>` styled
     `text-sm text-muted`. No DB column needed.
   - In `IndicatorsDashboard`, render `short` as a subtitle below the indicator value.

2. **"¿Qué significa esto para ti?" contextual block.**
   Below the explanation, a collapsible section with 2-3 concrete implications.
   No political framing. Just math and sourced facts.

   **Implementation:** Use a `<details>/<summary>` element styled to match DESIGN.md.
   Do NOT use `SectionTabs` — that component is for page navigation, not expandable content.
   Summary text: "¿Qué significa esto para ti?" — body contains 2-3 bullet points.

3. **Historical comparison view (only if salario_medio pre-condition passes).**
   "IPC acumulado desde 2020 vs salario medio acumulado" — a two-series line chart
   showing whether wages have kept up with inflation.

   **Implementation note:** Verify whether the existing `IndicatorChart` component
   supports multiple data series. If it only accepts `data: {period, value}[]` for a
   single series, this step requires either:
   - Extending the component with a `series: {label, data}[]` prop (preferred), or
   - Creating a dedicated `ComparisonChart` component.
   Treat this as M-effort (not S) if component surgery is needed. Do not rush this step.
   If `salario_medio` data is not available or chart work is too complex, skip and log
   it in NEXT.md as a gap.

4. **Shareable per-indicator URLs.**
   Verify that `/indicadores/[code]` already serves as a shareable deep link (it should —
   it's a Next.js dynamic route). If confirmed, add a copy-link icon to each indicator
   card on the index page and to the detail page that copies the `/indicadores/[code]` URL
   to clipboard. No new route or query-param scheme needed.

### What NOT to build in Phase A
- No editorial commentary on which party is "responsible."
- No personal salary input forms (privacy surface, not needed for the core value).
- No new data pipelines.

### Acceptance
- `npm run content:audit` passes (no editorial language in explanations).
- `npm run ui:audit` passes.
- Mobile readable: each explanation is legible on 375px viewport.
- Plain-language gate (pre-PR, not automated): read the IPC explanation aloud to a
  non-economist. They should understand it without asking questions. Record pass/fail
  in the PR description.

## Phase B — Data Closure: Declarations + SEPI

**Goal:** Close the accountability gap. A politician page should show what they declared,
what they approved, and what organizations they sat on.

### Declarations (bienes e ingresos)

The `etl/src/congreso/declaraciones.py` pipeline exists but is incomplete.
The Congreso publishes PDFs of income and asset declarations.

**What to build:**
- Extend the existing pipeline to extract structured fields from declaration PDFs:
  total income, real estate holdings, financial assets, by year.
- Store in `economic_declarations` table (already exists in schema).
- Surface on `/diputados/[id]` under a "Declaraciones" tab: what they declared,
  year by year, with source link to the official PDF.
- Empty state: "No hay declaración registrada para esta legislatura." with link
  to official source.

**Hard pre-condition (before estimating Phase B effort):**
Run a sample of 20 declaration PDFs (4 legislators × 5 years) through the existing
`declaraciones.py` pipeline. Classify failures by format. Report the number of distinct
PDF formats. Only commit to Phase B scope after this audit — if there are more than 5
distinct formats, the timeline is 4-8 weeks, not 2-4.

**Known constraints:**
- PDF format varies by politician and legislature. Parser must be resilient to format drift.
- Use the `--dry-run` flag pattern for CI smoke checks.
- Async and resumable (`--resume` flag). Declaration scraping can take hours.

### SEPI + State Company Boards

The `etl/src/instituciones/instituciones.py` handles the SEPI holding body.
The gap: SEPI subsidiary boards (Navantia, Correos, Renfe, etc.) are not covered at all.

**Pre-condition:** Before writing code, verify whether SEPI subsidiaries publish board
composition in machine-readable HTML or only as PDFs. Check sepi.es for each target entity.
- If HTML: extend `instituciones_nombramientos.yml` with scraped data.
- If PDFs only: fall back to manual YAML entry for the initial 10 entities, then build
  a scraper in a follow-up task.

**What to build:**
- Add YAML entries for 10+ major SEPI subsidiaries to `instituciones_nombramientos.yml`,
  following the existing format (see current entries for TC/CGPJ as the schema reference).
- Fuzzy-match to `politicians` table (same 0.85 confidence threshold already in use).
- Surface on `/organizaciones/[id]` for each state company.

**Example YAML entry format (for reference):**
```yaml
- name: Navantia
  type: empresa_publica
  parent: SEPI
  source_url: https://www.navantia.es/es/empresa/gobierno-corporativo/
  members:
    - name: [NOMBRE COMPLETO]
      role: Presidente
      start_date: "2023-01-01"
```

**Acceptance:**
- `PYTHONPATH=src python -m pytest tests/` passes for updated pipelines.
- Declaration parser handles at least 3 different PDF formats without crashing.
- SEPI subsidiary coverage: at least 10 major entities with current board data.
- Every new data point shows source URL and ETL run date.

## Phase B.5 — Judicial Accountability: corruption proceedings + active contracts

Before starting the full entity graph, ship this judicial-accountability slice. It uses the same
person/company/contract foundations Phase C needs, but has stricter source and review rules.

**Goal:** Let a citizen or researcher check whether a company, executive, or public office holder
appears in a sourced corruption-related proceeding, and whether that actor is connected to active
contracts or subsidies already in the portal.

This is not an opinion layer. The UI must show only procedural facts: status, offence category,
court/body, dates, source URL, and reviewed links to people, organizations, contracts, subsidies,
and municipalities.

### Source baseline

- Primary source: CGPJ "Repositorio de datos sobre procesos por corrupción", including reusable
  files where available. It covers proceedings with apertura de juicio oral or procesamiento,
  final convictions, and prison population for corruption-related offences. Updated quarterly.
- Secondary source for named case detail: CENDOJ/official judgment pages, BOE where applicable,
  court press notes, and official institutional releases. Media can be a discovery aid only, not
  the final source for public rows.

### What to build

1. **Schema and ETL foundation.**
   Add `etl/src/judicial/` and migrations for:
   - `corruption_cases`: source, court/body, offence category, procedural status, dates, summary,
     source URL, last verified.
   - `corruption_case_actors`: person or organization label, role in proceeding, optional
     `politician_id`, optional `organization_id`, match confidence, review status.
   - `corruption_contract_links`: reviewed links between a case actor and `contracts`,
     `subsidies`, or an `organization_id`, with evidence URL and link reason.

2. **Review gate for named actors.**
   Fuzzy matching may create candidates, but public rows require human review. A record must not
   appear on `/organizaciones/[id]`, `/diputados/[id]`, `/contratos/[id]`, or search until the
   actor link is reviewed.

3. **Public surfaces.**
   - `/corrupcion`: factual index of sourced proceedings and aggregate coverage.
   - `/corrupcion/[id]`: case detail with procedural status, offences, source links, reviewed
     actors, and linked contracts/subsidies.
   - `/organizaciones/[id]`: "Procedimientos relacionados" section when reviewed organization
     links exist.
   - `/contratos/[id]`: factual notice only when the contractor or awarding organization has a
     reviewed link to a proceeding.

4. **Phase C integration.**
   The future entity summary must include reviewed judicial links alongside contracts, subsidies,
   EU funds, SEPI boards, declarations, and revolving-door records.

### Status vocabulary

Use only these UI labels unless a source requires a more precise factual phrase:
- `procesamiento_o_juicio_oral`
- `condena_no_firme`
- `condena_firme`
- `absuelto`
- `sobreseido`
- `desconocido`

Never label a person or company as "corrupto". Do not infer guilt from investigation status.

### Acceptance

- `npm run content:audit` passes with no judgemental corruption copy.
- Unreviewed actor matches are not publicly readable.
- Every public case row has a source URL, source type, last verified date, and procedural status.
- A reviewed organization with an active contract can be reached from both the organization page
  and the contract page.
- Judicial ETL has a dry-run mode and tests for status mapping, review gating, and link creation.

## Phase C — Unified Entity Graph

**Goal:** Searching any company or person returns one view connecting everything:
contracts, subsidies, EU funds, officials who approved them, institutional roles, declarations.
The sentence this phase must make possible: "This company received 3.2M€ in contracts approved
by the Ministry of Industry, whose secretary of state between 2020-2023 was [name], who
previously sat on this company's board." Everything else is infrastructure.

**Prerequisites:**
- Phase B complete (declarations + SEPI data sources).
- Phase B.5 judicial-accountability tables and review gate exist if corruption/contract links are
  included in the first public entity graph release. If not, the entity graph must show the data
  gap explicitly instead of implying judicial coverage.
- Revolving door data: at least one `revolving_door` record with a matching `organization_id`
  must exist in production before the cross-entity link can be surfaced. Verify before starting.
- BORME integration (company director registry) is NOT required for Phase C — the entity
  graph will surface revolving door links already in the `revolving_door` table only.
  BORME is a post-C enhancement.

**Architecture note (mandatory, not optional):**
A `v_entity_summary` materialized view is required to avoid N+1 queries on the cascade page.
Supabase Free pauses after inactivity — cold starts on a multi-table join can take 5-8s.
The 2s target is for warm connections only. Cold-start behavior must be handled gracefully
in the UI (loading skeleton + "Cargando datos..." state). Do not promise sub-2s cold starts.

**This phase will be fully specced after Phase B ships.** The architecture may change
based on what Phase B reveals about data shape and join complexity. Do not over-design now.

**Rough acceptance (to be refined):**
- Searching "Navantia" returns contracts, subsidies, EU funds, board members, revolving door
  links — all in one page.
- Page loads under 2s on warm connection; handles cold start gracefully.
- `npm run lint && npm run build` passes. Mobile readable.

### Architecture findings

- **Phase A data reality**: Only 3 IPC indicators exist (IPC, IPC_VAR_MENSUAL, IPC_VAR_ANUAL). ETL `ine/indicadores.py:8-30` defines them via INE API series IDs. No salario_medio, tasa_paro, deuda, or PIB data exists — these require new ETL pipelines with INE API schemas different from the IPC one.
- **IndicatorChart is single-series only** (`IndicatorChart.tsx` uses single `<Area dataKey="value">`). Multi-series comparison requires extending the component or creating a dedicated ComparisonChart. Step 3 (IPC vs salario_medio) is skipped for Phase A since salario_medio doesn't exist.
- **Phase B foundations exist but are hollow**: `economic_declarations` table has no structured columns (all in `raw_data jsonb`). `EconomicDeclarationList` component shows URLs/dates but no structured income/asset data. PDF parsing adds significant new structure.
- **Phase C architecture is correctly deferred**: `v_organization_public` view and org detail page already handle multi-dimension data. Materialized view design happens after Phase B reveals data shape.

### Code quality notes

- `IndicatorChart:93` uses `useId().replace(/:/g, "")` for gradient IDs — React 18 useId returns strings with colons. This works but is fragile if React changes the ID format.
- `IndicatorSparkline:18` `range = max - min || 1` — safe, but single-point datasets produce a degenerated path (all points at same coordinate).
- `EconomicDeclarationList` reads structured data from `raw_data.type` — fragile. Phase B should add proper columns or define a typed extraction layer.

### Test coverage

- ETL: 20 test files with fixtures (IPC, BDNS, contracts, BORME, budgets, etc.)
- Web: 3 test files (etl-pipelines.test.ts, source-footnote-format.test.ts, utils). No component tests, no data layer tests.
- Phase A adds 3-5 vitest tests for explanations + copy-link behavior.

### Performance

- `getIndicators()` (conexiones.ts:22) has no LIMIT. Currently safe at ~720 rows (3 indicators × 240 points). Add `.limit(500)` now.
- `getIndicatorPoints()` (conexiones.ts:32) already has `.limit(120)` — good.
- Both functions use `unstable_cache` with 1-hour ISR — correctly avoids hitting Supabase on every request.
- Phase C requires `v_entity_summary` materialized view (cold starts can hit 5-8s). Not a Phase A concern.
