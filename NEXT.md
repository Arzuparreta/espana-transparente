## Vision

> "I show you the relevant information the government does not want you to know.
> I help you understand what IPC, inflation, and public debt actually mean for your life."

España Transparente is two things simultaneously: an accountability tool for researchers and journalists,
and an economic literacy platform for citizens who never studied economics. Nobody else has built this
combination. The official transparency portal (`transparencia.gob.es`) is a compliance instrument, not
a product. The INE publishes numbers nobody can read. This project bridges both gaps.

---

## Problem Statement

Public money in Spain is traceable in theory and opaque in practice. Contracts, subsidies, EU funds,
asset declarations, and institutional appointments are scattered across incompatible official sources.
Meanwhile, the economic indicators that affect every citizen's daily life (IPC, salario medio, deuda
pública) are published as raw tables that require economics training to interpret.

---

## What Makes This Compelling

- The only Spanish portal that connects money → officials → organizations in one searchable place.
- The only place that tells your dad what IPC actually means for his rent and salary.
- All from official public sources — no editorializing, no partisan framing.

---

## Constraints

- Supabase Free (500 MB). No new heavy tables without auditing current usage first.
- `transparencia.gob.es` is the government's own portal — Spain has a documented pattern of
  labeling accountability journalism as partisan. Editorial neutrality is non-negotiable.
- Congress portal rate-limits at 1.5s/request. Declaration PDF parsing must be async and resumable.
- Vercel Hobby deployment. No server-side heavy compute.

## Phase D — Information Architecture redesign (ACTIVE NEXT PHASE)

Decided 2026-05-25, approved. This is the next major body of work after the data-coverage
phases (A–C). It is pure IA/UI — **no new data pipeline needed**. No time pressure: the
correct redesign was chosen over the fast patch.

> **REVISION 2026-05-25 — "persona primero".** The thread spine shipped, but two of the
> abstract-concept threads (`Poder`, `Integridad`) proved unintuitive and — worse —
> *ontologically wrong*: `Poder` ("¿Quién manda y cómo decide?") attributed agency to
> aggregates (Gobierno, Senado, Partidos), and institutions were cross-listed as
> "Organismos de control" (an actor). This contradicts the core thesis (AGENTS.md §1:
> "cada agregado se muestra como un conjunto de personas, no un ente con voluntad propia").
> **Governing principle adopted: top-level categories name *things*, not *agencies*.**
> Persons are the only agents; everything else is a thing (money, a law, a number, a place)
> or a container of persons (organization, institution, party).
>
> Consequences (current IA, supersedes the table below):
> - **"Personas" returns as a top-level category** — reversing the earlier "Personas stops
>   being a top-level group" decision. It absorbs the former `Poder` + `Integridad` as three
>   sub-groups: **Cargos** (diputados, senado, gobierno, partidos, nombramientos) ·
>   **Decisiones** (votaciones, iniciativas) · **Conducta** (declaraciones, puertas
>   giratorias, procesos judiciales).
> - Top-level threads are now three concrete nouns: **Personas · Dinero · Economía**.
> - `instituciones` lives once, under Personas/Cargos as "Nombramientos" (no more
>   "Organismos de control" duplicate). `fondos-ue` is canonical under Dinero only.
>   `organizaciones` stays under Dinero as a recipient (a thing, not an actor).
> - `/poder` and `/integridad` redirect permanently to `/personas`. Leaf URLs unchanged.

### The problem

Data coverage has outrun the interface. ~20 datasets now exist, but the IA is organized
around the data model (17 source-named sections in 3 groups: Personas / Dinero y leyes /
Conexiones y contexto) instead of around what a visitor wants to know. A first-time visitor
cannot find what they came for. The richest asset — the EntityTrail graph (money → person →
org → case) — is buried inside detail pages, invisible from the front door. The fix is an
**intent layer on top of the source data**, not a reskin.

### Audience priority (locked)

1. **Citizen, "what affects me"** (theme-guided, no name in mind) — *most important*.
2. **Citizen, named-entity lookup** (arrives with a deputy / party / scandal) — almost equal.
3. **Researcher, follow-the-thread** — must be decently covered.

The front door leads with #1; the design serves all three by layer.

### Front door (locked)

Scrollytelling "state of the nation": a curated top-to-bottom narrative that teaches as you
scroll. Each anchor is a real number with plain-language meaning (deuda per cápita · qué es;
IPC del mes · qué significa para ti; el mayor contrato del mes). Editorial in *sequencing*,
strictly factual in *selection*.

### Chosen approach: threads as the spine

Reorganize the whole IA around ~5 durable citizen threads. The scrollytelling home crowns
them; each thread is a curated narrative-plus-explore landing weaving several datasets;
source pages become the deep layer reachable *within* a thread; EntityTrail is the
connective tissue. Top-level nav: 17 items → ~5 threads + persistent search.

| Thread | Citizen question | Primary datasets (deep layer) |
|---|---|---|
| **Dinero** | ¿A dónde va el dinero público? | trazabilidad, presupuestos, contratos, subvenciones, fondos UE, organizaciones (contrapartes) |
| **Economía** | ¿Cómo me afecta la economía? | indicadores (IPC, deuda, paro, salario, PIB) + explicaciones en lenguaje llano |
| **Integridad** | ¿Es honesto quien me representa? | declaraciones, puertas giratorias, procesos judiciales, nombramientos institucionales |
| **Poder** | ¿Quién manda y cómo decide? | gobierno, partidos, diputados, senado, votaciones, iniciativas |
| **Tu territorio** | ¿Qué pasa donde vivo? | gasto autonómico (CCAA), gasto municipal, tus representantes por provincia |

**"Personas" stops being a top-level group.** People and organizations become connective
*entities* that appear as actors inside every thread, reached primarily via global search
and via the threads where they act.

### Premises (agreed)

1. The redesign ADDS an intent layer; it does NOT delete source sections. Every source page
   survives as the deep layer. Richness preserved; only the entry changes.
2. **Curation by mechanical rule, never opinion.** Each anchor's selection rule is factual
   and sourced. The narrative teaches *what a number means*, never *asserts a scandal*.
   **Vote divergence is NOT a front-door anchor — deep-page / analytical only** (see fix
   note below and AGENTS.md §2).
3. **Named-entity lookup is a fixture, not a section:** persistent, instant global search
   from every screen.
4. **EntityTrail becomes both an embedded component AND a standalone surface** (out of the
   detail-page basement), but stays tier 3 — not the front door. Reuses existing trail
   queries; needs a non-graph (list/table) fallback for accessibility + small screens.
5. **Future data scope is governed by "does it complete a story we already tell," not "is it
   available."** New data must deepen an existing thread, not add a 21st directory item.
   *This supersedes "ingest what's available" as the scoping rule for all future data work
   (see the Data Gaps Register below — re-read it through this lens).*
